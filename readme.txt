The Old School Emulation Center
10/07/2023

Hello again folks and welcome to our bumper summer TOSEC release! In the 6
months since our last release we've been dragged kicking and screaming into the
21st Century, with our brand new Discord server helping bring more of the
community together and leading to some great new submissions and fixes. This
release sees substantial updates to every one of the branches and may be one of
our biggest ever!

Starting off in the main branch, we've got the usual flood of fixes and updates
to the Commodore C64 and Amiga systems from stalwarts duncantwain and Crashdisk.
It's a testament to the lasting appeal of these systems that they still have
incredibly active communities after all these years, and we're incredibly
grateful for the endless work that these two put in to keeping on top of the
flood. mictlantecuhtle, meanwhile, has added another few thousand files to the
Apple II DATs as well as reviewing the overall structure and streamlining the
way we deal with various disk image formats. As well as the usual swathe of
small updates to a range of obscure systems, this release also sees the addition
of a substantial number of entries added for Japanese platforms like the PC-88,
PC-98 and Sharp X68000. While mictlantecuhtle has done the renaming here, the
real credit belongs to krugman for their tireless work in dumping and preserving
these releases.

Rounding things off in this area, our very own master (mistress?) of the ZX
Spectrum, Lady Eklipse, is back with another comprehensive update to the system.
As usual there are far too many changes and additions to list sensibly here, so
you'll just have to download the DAT pack to see all the fantastic work that she
has put in for this release. We've also had some great submissions from users
new and old - thanks to Newpain and bikerspade in particular for their updates
and additions to systems in the Analogue and VTech V.Smile ranges, and a quick
shoutout to smesgr from the Discord for their Atari 2600 submissions.

Moving on to the other branches, after the unexpected update to the TOSEC-ISO
branch in January, the TOSEC-PIX team with the help of generous contributors
from the TOSEC Discord channel have released another huge update to the branch.
All new submissions dumped with DiscImageCreator or Redumper have been added to
the existing TOSEC-ISO database that was resurrected from the ashes of the now
defunct TOSEC-ISO website.

If the last TOSEC-PIX release shocked you then there's time for great alarm as
the TOSEC-PIX team have followed on with their second release (and probably
their largest release ever) in one year!! Included in this release are hundreds
of new scans and many contributions.

Lastly a big thank you to everyone that have joined the TOSEC Discord server and
to all the people that have reached out and provided submissions for this and
upcoming TOSEC releases.

With your help and support the project will continue to grow!

Be sure to join our Discord channel if you havenâ€™t already.

Enjoy this release and see you all in a few months!


************************************************************************

All folders and contents should be self-explanatory. All necessary
CUEs are also included. There are also some scripts included to assist
in creating TOSEC folders, and some 'move' scripts for those of you
using RomVault. As usual, be careful with long pathnames in some OSes.
Best advice is to use your folders as close as possible to the root of
the drive.

For all the latest work-in-progress news, as well as expanded details
on all upcoming changes and additions, keep up to date by visiting us
at: www.tosecdev.org

Enjoy!

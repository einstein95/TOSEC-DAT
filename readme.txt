The Old School Emulation Center
23/01/2023

Hello folks and welcome to our big "We missed all Anniversaries" release!

This one was initially supposed to be a Christmas release, which then slipped to New Year's, which then slipped to TOSEC's 23rd anniversary on the 18th of January, which then went by with nothing at all happening. Having missed all possible anniversaries due to our creaky old joints not being able to type fast enough, we are now happy to give you something on a totally unremarkable non-anniversary date.

The release itself, however, is definitely remarkable. Or at least that's what we think...
Duncantwain keeps going from strength to strength with his amazing C64 dats, coming up once again with numerous changes and improvements. This time his main focus was cleaning up and categorizing the Games section.

Crashdisk, always a guy you can depend on when it comes to the mighty Amiga, supplied several sparkling new Amiga dats that should keep everybody happy for a while.

Mictlantecuhtle always vouches for diversity and he has updated more than 150 dats, ranging all the way from the ancient Sinclair ZX81 to the very modern Sony PlayStation Vita.

We would also like to give a warm welcome to TOSEC newcomer FakeShemp! She provided a lot of new dats, containing mostly low level dumps like Flux and PCM recordings, which are definitely worth preserving and we are thankful for her help with that.

On the ISO front, Maddog continues his unending quest for dumping and documenting anything Dreamcast related. At least he did bother to provide some variety with some additional GameCube and Arcade dats. Unexpected help to the ISO branch came from the TOSEC PIX team (read below...), marking the first substantial dats we have published for Sony PlayStation 2 and PlayStation Portable.

Yes guys, it's real! The TOSEC-PIX team have finally awoken from their slumber after three long years without a major release.  For a few short weeks the teams' scanners were whirring, the keyboards were banging and while all this frivolity was going on, they even managed to break out the optical media and provide some dumps for TOSEC-ISO. Lets hope their next release does not take another 3 years.....

We hope you enjoy our "non-anniversary"(tm) release and we welcome any help and contributions you might have!

See you all in the next one!


************************************************************************

All folders and contents should be self-explanatory. All necessary
CUEs are also included. There are also some scripts included to assist
in creating TOSEC folders, and some 'move' scripts for those of you
using RomVault. As usual, be careful with long pathnames in some OSes.
Best advice is to use your folders as close as possible to the root of
the drive.

For all the latest work-in-progress news, as well as expanded details
on all upcoming changes and additions, keep up to date by visiting us
at: www.tosecdev.org

Enjoy!
